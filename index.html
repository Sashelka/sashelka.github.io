About

Bio

SASHELKA is a multidisciplinary visual artist working across painting, photography, and digital media. Her practice explores inner tension, feminine force, transformation, and the symbolic relationship between body, memory, and emotional states.

Through physically and digitally constructed imagery, she creates psychologically charged visual narratives shaped by contradiction, vulnerability, control, and metamorphosis. Her works often move between darkness and beauty, using symbolic objects, bodily forms, and surreal tension as recurring elements of her visual language.

Her work has been presented in exhibitions and digital art contexts internationally, while also existing within contemporary Web3 and NFT spaces as an extension of her broader artistic practice.

Artist Statement

My work is rooted in tension — between softness and control, beauty and rupture, vulnerability and force. I am drawn to images that carry contradiction within them: bodies that hold pressure, symbols that suggest both protection and threat, and scenes suspended between emotional silence and internal collapse.

Across painting, photography, and digital media, I use symbolic imagery to explore transformation, containment, instinct, and invisible psychological structures that shape human experience.

I return repeatedly to themes of feminine force, fracture, restraint, metamorphosis, and the unstable boundary between inner and outer states. For me, art is a way of materializing what cannot be spoken cleanly: internal conflict, transformation, psychological pressure, and survival through change.
