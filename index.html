About

Artist Statement

My work exists in the space between control and rupture. I am drawn to images that hold contradiction — beauty and discomfort, fragility and force, silence and pressure.

Across painting, photography, and digital media, I build symbolic scenes that speak through tension rather than explanation. I am interested in the emotional charge carried by bodies, objects, wounds, rituals, and suspended gestures.

I do not use symbolism as decoration. I use it as a structural language — to articulate what cannot be said directly, but can still be recognized immediately.

SASHELKA is a multidisciplinary visual artist working across oil painting, conceptual photography, and digital media.

Her practice explores transformation, memory, feminine force, internal tension, and psychological symbolism through dark, emotionally loaded visual narratives.

She creates works that merge intimacy with confrontation, building images that feel both elegant and unstable — suspended between attraction and unease.
